## Task 4: make FUSE opcode test PASS 100%

- Why the FUSE test is   PASS: 1340/1356 opcodes passed?

16 failures, all known/expected:

- 10 (DJNZ): Undocumented: FUSE expects the hidden WZ register to update; our FUSE core doesn't track WZ.
- 37 (SCF): Undocumented flag behavior (bits 3,5 of F from last ALU operation — FUSE tests an obscure edge case).
- cb46..cb7e (BIT n,(HL)): 12 failures — all BIT instruction variants testing undocumented flags (bits 3,5 of F). Real Z80s behave inconsistently here; the FUSE tests check one specific behavior that our core doesn't replicate exactly.
- dd00, ddfd00: DD/FD prefix followed by non-IX/IY opcode. Undocumented: FUSE expects these prefixes to consume a cycle and advance PC; our core treats them differently.

All 16 are undocumented Z80 behaviors — no real-world software depends on them. 1340/1356 (98.8%) is a normal result for the FUSE Z80 core.

Can you make our core be exactly 100% FUSE test compliant?
